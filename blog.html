1. Do dogs know their name?

Dogs are capable of learning and recognizing their names, especially with consistent training and reinforcement. They can associate the sound of their name with positive experiences, such as receiving treats or attention from their owners. However, their understanding may vary depending on factors like breed, age, and individual personality. Some dogs may respond more readily to their names than others, but in general, with proper training, dogs can learn to recognize and respond to their names.

2. Essential tips to raise a healthy dog:

Provide a balanced diet: Feed your dog high-quality, nutritionally balanced food appropriate for their age, size, and breed. Regular exercise: Ensure your dog gets enough physical activity to maintain a healthy weight, strengthen muscles, and prevent behavioral issues. Veterinary care: Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian for vaccinations, parasite control, dental care, and overall health assessments. Mental stimulation: Engage your dog with interactive toys, training sessions, and activities like puzzle games to keep their minds active and prevent boredom. Grooming: Regular grooming sessions, including brushing, nail trimming, and bathing, help maintain your dog's coat and skin health and prevent infections. Socialization: Expose your dog to different people, animals, and environments from an early age to promote good behavior and confidence in various situations.

3.Why does my dog attack me?

Dog aggression towards their owners can stem from various reasons, including fear, anxiety, resource guarding, territorial behavior, pain or discomfort, lack of socialization, past trauma, or frustration. It's essential to identify the underlying cause of the aggression to address it effectively. Consulting with a professional dog trainer or behaviorist can help develop a tailored behavior modification plan to manage and reduce aggressive tendencies in dogs.

4. How to throw a birthday party for your dog:

Serve dog-friendly treats: Offer a variety of dog treats and snacks made from safe ingredients suitable for canine consumption. Organize games and activities: Plan fun games like fetch, agility courses, or a doggy costume contest to keep the dogs entertained.
